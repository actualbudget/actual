Actual has grown a lot over the past few years, and our community has grown right alongside it. That growth is something to celebrate — but it has also attracted some bad actors who try to use all the hard work our volunteers have done to make a quick buck off the community, or in some cases to steal personal data. So we are introducing a new rule for our Discord server: **no advertising**.

<!--truncate-->

## Why We're Doing This

Actual is privacy oriented. That principle sits at the core of everything we build — your financial data stays yours, on your own infrastructure, under your own control.

As the community has grown, we have seen people use our Discord spaces to promote closed-source products built on top of Actual. Some of these are well-intentioned. Others are not. The trouble is that we have no way to tell the difference. When a product is built behind closed doors, we cannot inspect what it does with your data. We cannot verify that it respects your privacy. And we are not willing to lend the trust the community has placed in Actual to products we cannot stand behind.

The clearest example is the various mobile apps that people have built. Some of them ask you to hand over the credentials to your Actual instance, or to route your financial data through servers we know nothing about. We do not want our community spaces to be the place where users are funneled toward that kind of risk.

## Our Policy

From this point on, **advertising is not allowed in the Actual Discord**. This includes promoting paid products, closed-source apps, and services built on top of Actual.

Since we cannot verify what these products do with your data, we will not allow them to be advertised in spaces our community trusts.

## We Still Love Community Projects

To be clear: this is not about shutting down the community. We genuinely want to support the projects people are building around Actual.

The catch is the same spirit that drives Actual itself: free and open source. When a project is open source, anyone can read the code, see exactly what it does with your data, and trust it on its own merits. That is the kind of project we are happy to see the community build and share.

What we are pushing back on is the opposite — products built behind closed doors that use our community spaces purely as a free advertising channel, with no transparency about what happens to your data.

## Keeping the Community Safe

This rule exists for one reason: to protect the privacy and safety of our users. Discord has always been a welcoming place to ask questions, share ideas, and help one another get the most out of Actual. We want to keep it that way.

Thank you for helping us keep the community open, honest, and safe.
